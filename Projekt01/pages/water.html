Overview

Water-type Pokémon are versatile and one of the most abundant types in the Pokémon world. They are often associated with aquatic environments and have moves that mimic water's flow and power.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths: Water Pokémon are highly effective against Fire, Ground, and Rock types.

Weaknesses: They are vulnerable to Electric and Grass moves, which exploit their aquatic nature.

Fun Facts

  • The first Water Pokémon most players encounter is Squirtle, one of the original starter Pokémon.
  • Kyogre, a Legendary Pokémon, represents the ocean and is one of the most powerful Water types.
  • Water moves like Surf are iconic and useful both in battles and for traveling across water.
  • Many dual-type Pokémon pair Water with Flying, Ground, or Ice.
